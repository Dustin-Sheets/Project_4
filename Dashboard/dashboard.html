Heart Disease: The Silent Killer

Heart disease is among th eleading causes of Death in the United States amongst Men, Women, and most Ethinc groups. On average one person dies from a cardiovascular related disease every 33 seconds in the United States.

Heart Diseas in the South.

While heart diease runs rampant in the United States as a whole, it is most common in the South. On average, the Southern-most states are at a higher risk of high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and diabetes than the rest of the United States. We can contribute the increased risk to factors such as smoking, poor diet, lack of exercise, and obesity.

The different types of heart disease

    Coronary Artery Disease

    Coronary Artery Diseas is when fat deposits, also known as plaque, buildup in the walls of arteries that supply blood to the heart. Over time, this causes the arteries to narrow limiting the flow of blood to the heart.

    Valve Disease

    Heart Valve Disease is when one or more of your heart valves do not work well. Some people are born with heart valve disease as a congential heart defect, however others deveop it over time.

    Aneurysm

    An Aortic Aneurysm is a buldge the aorta. If the aneurysm ruptures, it can cause the walls of the artery to split allowing blood to leak in between them. One of the main causese of an aneurysm is the narrowing of the arteries resulting from coronary artery disease.

    Cardiac Arrhythmia

    Cardiac Arrhythmia occurs when the electrical signals that tell the heart to beat do not work. Some of the main causes of it are coronary artery diease, high blood pressure, and valve disorders

    Cardiomyopathy

    Cardiomyopathy is when the heart muscles make it harder for the heart to pump blood to the rest of the body. Like the other diseases, the main cause is coronary artery disease. Life style also plays a big role in developing the disease.

    Pericarditis

    Pericarditis is the swelling and irritation of the thin tissue surrounding the heart. Chronic illnesses such as lupus and rheumatoid arthritis are the leading cause.

    Heart Failure

    Heart Failure is when your heart does not pump enough blood to supply your body's needs. Coronary disease, high blood pressure, and valve diseas are among the leading causes of heart failure.
